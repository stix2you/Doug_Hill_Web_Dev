My Web Development Journey

I am transitioning to a Web Development career after two previous careers.

Pro Audio and Music

My first career began in the 90's, after receiving my Bachelor's degree in Music. During the day I worked selling Pro Audio Equipment Sales, working for a nationwide company called Full Compass Systems. Nights and weekends found me playing as a drummer and percussionist in several bands and recording projects. After college, I spent about 10 years completely immersed in this world before deciding to make a change to better support my family.

Auto Mechanics

For about the past 15 years I have been an active auto mechanic. I trained at Madison College (Madison, Wisconsin) from 2007 to 2009, and dove in the deep end by embracing "A" level auto repair work and advanced diagnostics from the very beginning of my professional career. As I grew within the field, I found myself drawn to the electrical and computer side of modern vehicles. I began to specialize in advanced electrical and computer diagnosics. At Foreign Car Specialists, we would receive vehicles which even the dealership had given up on, and tackled the computer module replacements, wiring harness repairs, vehicle network diagnostics, vehicle programming, and diagnostics which involved combining the mechanical world with the electrical world. When interfacing between the two, I often found myself with an oscilloscope in hand looking directly at signal going in and out of the computers in order to fault trace.

Web Development

That brings me to my foray into the world of Web Development. Having grown up in the 80's, learning Basic on an Apple //e, then C++ in the 90's on PC based machines, computers have always been a "hobby," much like auto repair always was from the moment I could pick up a wrench. I didn't come to the auto repair world without a solid foundation for mechanics, and it's a similar story with Web Development. I only recently discovered I was interested in Web Development when my best friend's son went to school for it. After seeing the kind of work he was doing, it seemed that a natural extension of the computer heavy work I was already doing with automobiles. I was totally hooked and began to seek out bootcamps, ending up finally at Career Foundry.

Since starting the program in October of 2023, I have been absolutely smitten with this world. I can't get enough of it, and every day feels more like an adventure than a job or school.
